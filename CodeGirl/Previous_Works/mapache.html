Yachay Math School Character

A mascot character design representing the Mathematics and Computer Science degree program at Yachay Tech University

Project Objective

Create a memorable mascot character that embodies the intersection of mathematics and computer science for Yachay Tech University's promotional materials.

Client

Yachay Tech University - Mathematics and Computer Science Department

Timeline

1 weeks (Concept to Final Delivery)

Collaboration

Developed in collaboration with:

Math Department CS Department

Final character design - "The Math Raccoon" holding a laptop with mathematical formulas

Final character design - "The Math Raccoon" holding a laptop with mathematical formulas

Design Process

1

Research & Brainstorming

Conducted meetings with university departments to understand the program's values. Researched animals associated with intelligence, curiosity, and problem-solving. Selected the raccoon for its cleverness and dexterous "hands".

2

Concept Sketches

Created multiple sketch variations exploring different poses, expressions, and accessories that represent both mathematics (formulas, calculators) and computer science (laptops, code snippets).

3

Client Feedback & Revision

Presented concepts to the university committee. Incorporated feedback to make the character more approachable while maintaining academic seriousness. Added subtle mathematical elements to the design.

4

Digital Illustration

Created the final digital illustration with clean lines, vibrant colors that match the university's brand palette, and details that work at various sizes (from social media icons to large prints).

Project Outcome

The "Math and Computer Science Raccoons" were successfully adopted in the Christmas Event of the Math and Computer Science School at Yachay Tech University.

Implementation & Usage:

  • Promotional Materials: Used in pines and flyers for the event

The raccoon characters have become recognizable mascots that effectively communicate the intersection of mathematics and computer science to prospective students. Their adoption in the Christmas event marked their successful integration into the university's visual identity.
